My Journaling Process

Step 1: Determine the Theme

Before I start making anything, I have to figure out the theme. I usually choose my theme based on current events, just like a diary. Some examples of themes would be the weather (maybe there was just a storm), any songs that recently came out, a tv show/movie, etc.

Step 2: Gather supplies

Once I have a theme decided, I can go through all my art supplies to pick what I need. These are the basics I usually pick from:

Printed pictures Magazine clippings Memo sheets
Washi tape Stickers Highlighters/markers
Pens Sticky notes Scissors/hole punch
Step 3: Glue in Pictures & Clippings

After I have what I need, it's pretty straightforward. Depending on the theme, I may layer clippings and pictures differently.

Step 4: Add Stickers & Washi Tape

When all the pictures are down, I basically have free reign to put as many stickers as I want, seeing as they're the topmost layer on the page. However, I usually lay down any washi tape I want first before stickers, and sometimes I even lay the tape down before any pictures.

Step 5: Do the Writing + Cleanup

The last step is to write in whatever I want. Sometimes it's a simple diary page, sometimes it's song lyrics or a poem - it depends. After that I trim off any excess tape and anything covering the holes in my journal pages (they're hole-punched to fit in a binder).

After all of that, the journal spread is done! That's my general process for journaling, but it's of course adjustable.
